# Biz Scout — Keep Private, Do Not Productize

## Situation

Biz Scout is running daily, scraping Sunbiz corporate filings, filtering for Polk County, and pushing to Google Sheets. It's a working lead gen pipeline. The question: should we package it as a paid product (one-time list purchase + subscription for weekly/daily updates)?

## Decision

No. Keep Biz Scout as a private competitive advantage. Do not sell the data.

## Why

1. **The data is free and public.** Sunbiz daily downloads are available to anyone. The barrier to entry is parsing fixed-width text files — not hard for anyone technical. Selling free public data is a race to the bottom.

2. **Competitors already exist.** BizLeadIndex sells exactly this for all of Florida. Apify has Sunbiz scrapers. We'd be entering a market with established players offering more coverage.

3. **Small TAM.** The buyer is a local service provider in Polk County who wants to reach new businesses — insurance agents, accountants, commercial lenders, payroll companies. Maybe 20-50 realistic buyers in the county. At $50-100/mo, that's $1K-5K/mo at full saturation.

4. **Support tax.** Data products attract complaints: wrong addresses, missing businesses, feature requests for other counties. Every subscriber becomes a mini client relationship — the opposite of leverage.

5. **Cannibalization.** Selling the list to other web designers or agencies hands our first-mover advantage to competitors.

## The Better Play

Use Biz Scout to feed our own direct mail pipeline via Lob. One closed website deal per month ($999-2,500) generates $12K-30K/year — more than subscriptions would ever produce, with zero support burden and zero competitor access to our data.

## What About Other Agents?

EventScout is the stronger productization candidate — broader appeal, harder to DIY, more defensible. See separate decision/research for that opportunity.
