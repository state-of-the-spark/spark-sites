# The SPARK Method — Book Framework

The proprietary 5-tier marketing framework for small businesses. Build from the bottom up. Most SMBs skip to the top and wonder why nothing works.

**Authors:** Grant Sparks, Nicole Bradham
**Format:** Premium hardbound book, $27 retail
**Audience:** Small business owners who are overwhelmed by marketing and spending more while trusting less

---

```
         K — KINDLE the fire
        (Paid ads, 27 impressions)
```

## Tier Details

### S — STAND for Something (Mindset)

**Felt pain:** "I'm doing everything myself and drowning" (50% of SMBs have zero marketing staff)

**Spark's solution:** Accept that YOU are the marketing department. Marketing is your #1 job — not something you do after the "real work" is done.

**AI layer:** AI makes your messaging CLEARER — not just faster. AI as a thinking partner for articulating what you actually do.

**What Grant has seen work (857 clients):** The businesses that grow are the ones where the owner accepts that marketing is the top priority of their role.

**Models to evaluate:** Mike Michalowicz's "Fix This Next" (hierarchy model), DISC temperament profiles (how personality affects marketing approach)

---

### P — POSITION Your Brand (Identity)

**Felt pain:** "I don't know what makes us different" / website as negative selection

**Spark's solution:** Personal brand + messaging + website that doesn't repel. You and your team ARE the brand.

**AI layer:** AI commoditized templates — your HUMAN identity matters more, not less. Template sites are dead. 74% of new websites feature AI-supported content. Standing out requires being human.

**What Grant has seen work (857 clients):** Realizing they (and their team) ARE the brand. Stand-out messaging. Website as trust signal — people won't NOT do business because of your site.

**Models to evaluate:** "Purple Cow" by Seth Godin (remarkable messaging), "Brand Hijack" (guerrilla branding), Donald Miller's StoryBrand (SB7 Framework), The Awareness Ladder

**Maps to Spark services:** Starter Site, website tiers, Strategy Session (Messaging)

---

### A — ATTRACT with Content (Content)

**Felt pain:** "Content creation is overwhelming" (34% cite this as a barrier)

**Spark's solution:** Daily content system that doesn't burn you out. Provide value in groups without selling. 10x your content output through repurposing.

**AI layer:** AI assists but human-led content wins. Creator-led content exceeds brand distinction benchmarks by 5x. The gap between AI-enabled and non-enabled businesses widens every quarter.

**What Grant has seen work (857 clients):** Regular daily content. 10x content output through repurposing. Providing value in groups and communities without selling.

**Models to evaluate:** Jab Jab Jab Right Hook (Vaynerchuk), Hook-Story-Offer, 3 content pillars, the 5-hour/week content system

**Maps to Spark services:** Content Bot ($1,500 setup + $99/mo), Social Media Management, Strategy Session (Social Media)

---

### R — REACH Your Market (Outreach)

**Felt pain:** "I need more customers consistently" (60% say finding new customers is #1 challenge)

**Spark's solution:** DMs + events + groups = systematic prospecting. Not hoping for referrals — actively building relationships.

**AI layer:** AI can scale outreach but authentic relationships still close deals. 83% of SMBs still rely on referrals — the ones who add systematic outreach to referrals are the ones who grow.

**What Grant has seen work (857 clients):** Direct DMs. Attending niche expos, meetups, live events that target clients attend. Networking with purpose, not just showing up.

**Models to evaluate:** Hormozi's Core Four from $100M Leads (Warm/Cold x One-to-One/One-to-Many), referral flywheels, the 3x3 engagement method

**Maps to Spark services:** Strategy Session (Funnel), Influencer Events, Event Scout agent

---

### K — KINDLE the Fire (Amplify)

**Felt pain:** "I'm spending money and can't tell if it's working" (only 18% of SMBs feel confident in marketing)

**Spark's solution:** Strategic awareness ads with the 27-impression framework. Paid amplification that builds on the foundation — not a replacement for it.

**AI layer:** AI-driven campaigns deliver 22% higher ROI and 32% more conversions — but only if the foundation (S-P-A-R) is right. Without identity, content, and outreach, ads just amplify noise.

**What Grant has seen work (857 clients):** Ongoing awareness ad campaigns on Meta and Google. The 27-impression rule — a prospect needs to see you 27 times before they take action.

**Models to evaluate:** The 27 Impressions framework, Hormozi's paid advertising quadrant, Google Ads vs. Meta Ads positioning

**Maps to Spark services:** Google Ads, Facebook Ads, Strategy Session (Ads), VIP Dashboards (sparkmycampaign.com)

## The Core Insight

**The book should make the reader smarter about marketing without making them a marketer.**

The gap between understanding and execution is where Spark's services live:
- **Knowledge gap:** The book closes this. Reader now knows the SPARK Method.
- **Implementation gap:** The book cannot close this. Reader still needs systems, teams, ads, offers.
- **Speed gap:** DIY takes 2 years. With Spark, it happens in 60-90 days.
- **Accountability gap:** Information without coaching = inconsistent execution.

## Book Structure (Mapped to SPARK)

### Part 1: The Problem (3-4 chapters)
1. **The Confidence Crisis** — 18% confidence stat. Spending more, trusting less.
2. **Why Most Marketing Advice Doesn't Work for SMBs** — Built for big budgets, big teams.
3. **What AI Changed (and What It Didn't)** — The trust paradox. Templates are dead. Human connection wins.
4. **What We've Seen Across 857 Clients** — Credibility chapter. The patterns that repeat.

### Part 2: The SPARK Method (5 chapters)
5. **S — Stand for Something** — Mindset: marketing is your #1 job
6. **P — Position Your Brand** — Identity: you ARE the brand
7. **A — Attract with Content** — Content: daily, value-first, human-led
8. **R — Reach Your Market** — Outreach: DMs, events, groups
9. **K — Kindle the Fire** — Amplify: strategic paid ads on a foundation

### Part 3: Implementation (3-4 chapters)
10. **First 30 Days** — Refined Quick Start Guide
11. **Common Mistakes** — From 857 real clients
12. **Case Studies** — 2-3 real before/after with numbers
13. **What's Next** — "If you want to do this yourself, you have everything. If you want help, here's how."

### Appendices
- Marketing scorecard / self-assessment
- Worksheets and templates
- Recommended tools (the 2-3 that matter)
