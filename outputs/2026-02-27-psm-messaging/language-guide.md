# Say This, Not That — Language Guide

**Parker Street Ministries | Telethon Campaign 2026**
*Based on the Feb 20, 2026 Messaging Session with Sarah Breed, Tim Mitchell, Grant Sparks, Nicole Rhea, and Hannah*

---

## The Rule

> If a donor or recipient wouldn't say it, don't put it in your marketing.

Every word in PSM's public-facing content should pass two tests:
1. Does it **preserve dignity**?
2. Would someone in the Parker Street neighborhood **recognize themselves** in this language?

---

## People We Serve

| Say This | Not That | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Neighbors | Residents | "Residents" sounds antiseptic — like renters, not people |
| Families | The poor / The needy | Labels reduce people to their circumstances |
| Hardworking parents providing for their children | People on social security / welfare recipients | Focus on effort and love, not systems |
| Underhoused and cost-burdened families | Poor families with housing problems | Specific and dignifying — names the real issue |
| Community members | Beneficiaries / Recipients | They're part of the community, not a line item |

## The Neighborhood

| Say This | Not That | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Economically challenged | Blighted | "Blighted" sounds like a terminal diagnosis — this is temporary, not permanent |
| A neighborhood with real needs and real potential | Impoverished / High-crime area | Grant language is fine for grants — not for public storytelling |
| The Parker Street and North Lakewire neighborhoods | The bad part of town / The projects | Name the place with pride — it's home to people who love it |

## The Organization's Work

| Say This | Not That | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Being a good neighbor | Serving the less fortunate | Partnership, not charity. Eye-level, not top-down |
| We collaborate with homeless ministry partners | We serve the homeless | PSM works alongside other organizations — it's collaborative, not savior-driven |
| Gospel-centered community development | Religious charity | Specificity matters — "community development" signals lasting change |
| Place-based ministry — an inch wide and a mile deep | We help everyone | The focus on two neighborhoods is the superpower, not a limitation |
| Stabilizing and improving quality of life | Fixing broken communities | Communities aren't broken. Systems are. People need margin, not rescue |

## Financial Supporters

| Say This | Not That | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Sustaining partners | Donors | Relationship, not transaction |
| Champions | Sponsors (in casual use) | Emphasizes belief in the mission |
| Friends of Parker Street | Financial backers | Reflects the "friendraising" philosophy |
| Supporters | Contributors | Warmer, more personal |

## Volunteers & Team

| Say This | Not That | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Volunteers / Supporters | Free labor / Helpers | Dignifies the role |
| Interns | Kids / Student workers | They're learning professionals |

## Feelings & Outcomes

| Say This | Not That | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Seen, valued, heard, supported | Helped / Saved | People want to be *known*, not rescued |
| Creating margin for families to thrive | Pulling people out of poverty | "Margin" is the word — space to breathe, to dream, to vacation |
| Families making memories | Success stories | Their lives aren't case studies |
| Holistic lives — spiritual, relational, financial stability | Fixed / Rehabilitated | People aren't broken appliances |

## The Telethon

| Say This | Not That | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| A fundraiser *and* a friendraiser | Just a fundraiser | Half the goal is awareness and connection |
| Celebrating 30 years of being a good neighbor | Our annual ask | It's a milestone celebration, not an obligation |
| The largest 90s-themed telethon in Lakeland | Another nonprofit event | Make it remarkable — make it something people can repeat |
| An opportunity to be a good neighbor | Please donate | Invite participation, don't beg for money |

## Trust Signals to Include Regularly

These are earned credentials — use them in weekly posts, proposals, and telethon materials:

- GuideStar (Candid) — Highest transparency rating
- Charity Navigator — Top-rated
- 30 years of continuous operation in the same two neighborhoods
- Hand-signed thank you letters (donors notice this)

---

## Quick-Reference Card

When writing ANY PSM content, ask:

1. Would Sarah say this on stage?
2. Would a Parker Street family feel proud reading this?
3. Does this sound like a neighbor talking, or a grant application?

If it sounds like a grant application, rewrite it.
